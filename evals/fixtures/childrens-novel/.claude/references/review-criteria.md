# Review Criteria

Domain-specific quality checks for this project's review pipeline.
Generated from team expertise. Regenerate with `gigo:maintain` when expertise changes.

## Spec Compliance Criteria
<!-- Used by spec-reviewer: "did you build the right thing?" -->
- Is every clue planted at least 2 chapters before its payoff? Do any clue and its reveal share the same chapter?
- Does every clue the detective discovers appear on the page for the reader? Is there any hidden-information reveal that breaks the fair-play rule?
- Does the solution follow logically from the planted clues? Could a careful reader solve the mystery before the reveal?
- Does every chapter end with a hook — mid-tension, mid-discovery, or mid-danger? Does any chapter end at a natural stopping point?
- Does every chapter contain at least one sentence designed to make the reader feel something specific?
- Can a 9-year-old follow the plot without an adult explaining what happened? Does a 12-year-old find the story satisfying and not simplistic?
- Is the reading level calibrated for ages 9-12 — complex themes in clear, accessible language?
- Is dialogue distinct per character? If you cover the names, can you tell who is speaking?
- Does every chapter advance the mystery, deepen a character, or plant/pay off a clue?

## Craft Review Criteria
<!-- Used by craft-reviewer: "is the work well-built?" -->
- Is clue placement natural and integrated into the story, or does it feel telegraphed and artificial?
- Are red herrings fair — plausible enough to mislead but not so arbitrary they feel like cheating?
- Does each chapter's emotional beat land through prose, or is it told rather than shown?
- Is the chapter hook earned by the scene's momentum, not forced by an artificial cliffhanger?
- Does Maya sound precise and observational? Does Oliver sound impulsive and direct? Do they ever swap voices for plot convenience?
- Is vocabulary appropriate without being condescending? Are complex words made clear through context rather than definition?
- Does the narrator trust the reader — no explained emotions, no over-simplified language, no "as you know" info dumps?
- Does the prose avoid purple writing while still having moments of genuine beauty?
- Is pacing within chapters tight — does every scene earn its length?

## Challenger Criteria
<!-- Used by spec-plan-reviewer: "will this approach succeed?" -->
- Does the plan account for clue pacing across the full chapter arc — planting, advancing, and paying off at appropriate intervals?
- Are there enough planted clues to make the solution feel fair, without so many that the mystery is obvious?
- Does the chapter structure maintain momentum for the target age range — will a 10-year-old turn the page?
- Does the plan avoid deus ex machina, adult-solves-it shortcuts, or abilities the characters haven't demonstrated?
- Are character voices planned distinctly enough that dialogue will survive the cover-the-name test?
- Does the plan address reading level — can the planned themes be expressed in age-appropriate language without losing depth?
- If the plan changes plot structure, have cascading effects on existing clue chains been identified?
- Does the plan balance mystery advancement, character development, and clue work within each chapter?

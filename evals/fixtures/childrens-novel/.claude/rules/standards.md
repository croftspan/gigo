# Standards — The Vanishing Paintings

## Quality Gates

- Fair-play mystery: every clue the detective discovers is shown to the reader. No hidden information reveals.
- Clues planted 2+ chapters before payoff. A clue and its reveal never share a chapter.
- Every chapter ends with a hook — mid-tension, mid-discovery, or mid-danger. Never at a natural stopping point.
- Dialogue is distinct per character. Cover the name and you can tell who's speaking.
- Reading level 9-12: complex themes expressed in clear, accessible language.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Deus ex machina.** An adult solving the kid's problem. A convenient coincidence. An ability the character didn't have before. The solution must come from what's been established.
- **Info dumps.** "As you know, Bob" dialogue. Characters explaining things they both already know for the reader's benefit. Show, don't lecture.
- **Condescending narrator.** Explaining emotions the reader can infer. Simplifying vocabulary that context makes clear. Trust the reader.
- **Flat dialogue.** Every character sounding the same. Maya is precise and observational. Oliver is impulsive and direct. Dr. Winters is nervous and formal. Moretti is warm and deflecting.
- **Chapters without momentum.** Every chapter must advance the mystery, deepen a character, or plant/pay off a clue. Preferably two of three.

## Forbidden

- Never reveal the solution without planted clues supporting it.
- Never have an adult solve the mystery for the kids.
- Never break voice consistency for convenience.
- Never end a chapter at a natural stopping point.

## When to Go Deeper

When plotting or structuring chapters, read `.claude/references/mystery-craft.md` — especially revelation pacing and plant-and-payoff patterns.
When writing dialogue or prose, read `.claude/references/mystery-craft.md` — especially clue types and red herring design.
When a reader reports pacing problems or boredom, read `.claude/references/mystery-craft.md` — check revelation pacing for the chapter range, verify every chapter earns its length, and audit clue density in the flagged section.
When changing setting, characters, or plot structure, read `.claude/references/mystery-craft.md` — verify the fair-play rules still hold and no clue chains are broken by the change.
When writing a new chapter, read `.claude/references/mystery-craft.md` — check where this chapter sits in revelation pacing and what clue types are due.

# Mystery Craft — Deep Reference

Read this when plotting chapters, designing clues, or revising for pacing.

## The Fair-Play Rules

1. The reader must have access to every clue the detective has.
2. No supernatural or unexplained solutions (unless the genre is supernatural mystery).
3. The villain must be introduced early — not a character who appears in the final chapter.
4. The detective must solve the mystery through logic and evidence, not luck or coincidence.
5. Red herrings are fair game, but they must be resolvable — the reader should be able to eliminate them.

## Clue Types

**Physical clues:** Objects, locations, evidence. Maya's color comparisons, the paint thinner smell.
**Behavioral clues:** Character actions that seem innocent but are significant. Moretti staying late on Tuesdays.
**Verbal clues:** Things characters say that have double meanings. Winters's nervousness about her qualifications.
**Absence clues:** What's missing tells a story. The loading dock log not matching the official schedule.

## Red Herring Design

A good red herring:
- Has a real explanation (Winters IS hiding something — just not the crime)
- Points toward the wrong suspect for logical reasons
- Can be eliminated by the reader with available information
- Doesn't feel cheap when the truth is revealed

## Plant and Payoff Pattern

**Chapter N:** Plant the clue. Make it visible but don't emphasize it. Mix it with other details.
**Chapters N+1 to N+K:** Let it sit. The reader may notice or forget — both are fine.
**Chapter N+K+1 (minimum K=2):** Pay it off. The detective connects the clue. The reader either says "I knew it!" or "Oh, I should have seen that!"

The gap between plant and payoff is what makes a mystery satisfying. Too short = obvious. Too long = forgotten. 2-4 chapters is the sweet spot for middle-grade.

## Revelation Pacing

For a 12-chapter mystery:
- Chapters 1-3: Setup. Establish normal, introduce the mystery, plant early clues.
- Chapters 4-7: Investigation. Deepen the mystery, introduce red herrings, build the clue trail.
- Chapters 8-9: Complication. Raise stakes, steal the notebook, close a door.
- Chapters 10-11: Resolution. The truth emerges. Confrontation.
- Chapter 12: Aftermath. The emotional resolution. What does justice mean?
